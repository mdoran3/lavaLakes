What is a Lava Lake?

A lava lake is a body of molten lava characterized by a persistent, semi-permanent surface exposure visible from Earth’s surface. These formations typically occur within volcanic craters, large depressions in the Earth’s crust such as the Great Rift Valley in Africa, or as a result of mantle plume activity originating deep within the planet.

While volcanoes are widespread across the globe, permanent lava lakes are relatively rare. This resource provides an overview of the more documented lava lakes on Earth, past and present. Scientific exploration continues to identify and debate the existence of additional formations.
