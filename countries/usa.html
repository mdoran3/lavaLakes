Kilauea

Klauea is an active shield volcano on Hawaii's Big Island. Scientists believe a mantle plume or hotspot exists directly below the island. When the plume is active, the island spills out more molten lava and creates new land. Combining the movement of the Pacific plate on which the base of the island sits with the interval timing of mantle plume activity, a chain of islands has been formed over time with the oldest Hawaiian Islands in the northwest and the youngest in the southeast. This was also once the sight of Hawaii's largest fresh water lakes, until an eruption by Kilauea occured and its lava boiled it into the atmosphere and filled the basin. Today Kilauea's lava lake inside the Halema'uma'u crater is viewed from safe distances by tourists from all over the world.
